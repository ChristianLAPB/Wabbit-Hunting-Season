This game is a hunting simulator, specifically to catch rabbits (cause its easter). What you do is you can put up to 5 traps anywhere on the map that isn't on the rabbits home or their food to try and catch them. Your goal is to catch all 5 rabbits before the timer runs out. Once the game is over, you can play again. 

The topics I included in this project was wander, path following, dijkstra, state machines, perlin noise and pseudorandom generation.

Wander is used to allow the bunnies to travel freely to find food. Path following is used to follow a path that has been predetermined for the rabbit to follow to get to food, water or home. Dijkstra is used to create said path for the quickest route to water or home. State machines are used to switch the rabbits between wandering around, eating a carrot, looking for and drinking water, looking for home and sleeping and being trapped. Perlin noise is used to help generate the terrain, using the values to dictate where the water, forests and mountains appear randomly each time. Pseudorandom generation is used to randomly place the home and food tiles on any empty tile that isn't occupied by water, forest, mountains, a home or food. The user interaction is allowing the player to click anywhere on the screen that isn't water or food/home to put a trap down.
